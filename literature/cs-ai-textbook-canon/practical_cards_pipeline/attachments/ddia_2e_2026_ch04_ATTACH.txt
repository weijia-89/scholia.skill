## scope

Chapter 4 explains **how databases store and retrieve data** (lines 5478–7528)—the storage-engine layer beneath Ch. 3 query models. Major arcs:

1. **OLTP storage** — Bash key-value toy DB; hash indexes; log-structured storage (SSTables, compaction, LSM-trees); B-trees and update-in-place; write amplification; secondary indexes.

2. **OLAP storage** — Column-oriented layouts; compression; sort order; vectorized execution; cloud warehouses (Snowflake, BigQuery, Redshift).

3. **Advanced indexes** — Multidimensional (R-trees, geospatial); **full-text search** (inverted index, postings lists, Lucene/Elasticsearch); **vector embeddings** (semantic search, flat/IVF/HNSW indexes, Faiss/pgvector).

Explicit OLTP vs OLAP split from Ch. 1. **Primary w1 RAG pairing chapter** with `hands_on_llms_2024` Ch. 8 and `ai_engineering_2025` Ch. 6. [verified, lines 5478–7528]
