## key_findings

All claims below are **[verified from text]** unless tagged `[contested]` or `[inferred]`.

### Multimodality and ViT

- Models accepting multiple modalities (text, image, audio, video, sensors) are **multimodal**; input modality ≠ output modality (lines 9489–9500).
- ViT generalizes Transformer success from NLP to vision; outperforms default CNNs on image recognition (Dosovitskiy et al. 2020) (lines 9525–9534).
- Images tokenized by **patching** (e.g., 16×16); patches linearly embedded; encoder treats patch embeddings like textual tokens — no architectural difference after embedding (lines 9554–9591).
- ViT commonly used to make language models multimodal, especially during embedding-model training (lines 9593–9595).

### CLIP and OpenCLIP

- CLIP (Radford et al. 2021): dual encoders on image-caption pairs; **contrastive learning** maximizes cosine similarity for matched pairs, minimizes for mismatched; negatives essential (lines 9640–9717).
- CLIP enables zero-shot classification (image vs class-description embeddings), clustering, cross-modal search, and conditioning generative models (e.g., Stable Diffusion) (lines 9648–9663).
- OpenCLIP workflow: separate text/image preprocessing; `get_text_features` / `get_image_features` → same 512-dim shape; normalize then dot product for similarity (lines 9721–9893).
- CLIP uses `<|startoftext|>` / `<|endoftext|>`; [CLS] represents image embedding in CLIP tokenizer design (lines 9804–9807).
- sentence-transformers `clip-ViT-B-32` reduces CLIP usage to encode + `util.cos_sim` (lines 9912–9932).

### BLIP-2 and visual LLMs

- Text-only LLMs (Llama 2, ChatGPT) cannot natively reason about images; multimodal embeddings and bridges extend capability (lines 9935–9948).
- BLIP-2 (Li et al. 2023): **Q-Former** is sole trainable bridge between frozen ViT and frozen LLM — avoids training billions of image-text pairs from scratch (lines 9962–10051).
- Stage 1 Q-Former tasks: image-text contrastive, image-text matching (binary), image-grounded text generation — jointly optimize visual representations from frozen ViT (lines 10006–10028).
- Stage 2: learnable embeddings → linear projection → **soft visual prompts** conditioning LLM (lines 10030–10051).
- LLaVA (visual instruction tuning), Idefics 2 (Mistral 7B): same pattern — CLIP-like encoder + LLM projection (lines 10057–10065).
- BLIP-2 example model: `Salesforce/blip2-opt-2.7b`; processor resizes to 224×224 squares — wide/tall images may distort (lines 10077–10132).
- Captioning: `model.generate(**inputs)` → decode; domain-specific images (cartoons, imaginary) may fail on public-data training (lines 10193–10247).
- Visual QA: prompt template `Question: ... Answer:`; multi-turn chat by concatenating prior Q/A in prompt; ipywidgets interactive chatbot (lines 10287–10422).
- Chat example: cost follow-up yields highly specific but unreliable answer (`$1,000,000`) — illustrates hallucination risk in VQA (lines 10340–10357).

### Chapter close

- Multimodality enables captioning, search, chat-based image reasoning; Ch. 10 covers contrastive embedding training in depth (lines 10424–10454).
