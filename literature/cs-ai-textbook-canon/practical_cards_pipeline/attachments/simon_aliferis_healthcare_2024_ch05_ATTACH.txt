## Scope

Selective ingest **05** spans book **Chapter 8** (*Data Preparation, Transforms, Quality, and Management*, L25038–27305) and book **Chapter 9** (*Evaluation*, L27308–30520). Primary title: **Data Preparation & Management**; Evaluation is bundled inside the line slice and completes the metrics/error-estimation canon referenced throughout the book.

**Part A — Data preparation (book ch. 8).** Two-step pipeline (Fig. 1): **Prepare Data** (raw → data-design features → data mart + quality) then **Feature Engineering** (design features → ML features), both iterative with backward steps to new raw sources; supported by a **Data Ecosystem** (mapping, tools, lineage, version control).

**Working with data:** EHR-centric realities — local terminologies, units, quality defects; definitions of **concept**, **vocabulary/terminology**, **ontology**; **dataset**, **research data warehouse**, **data mart** (project-specific, standardized, linked via data model); **data dictionary**; **common data models** (OMOP CDM, N3C, eMerge).

**Constructing design features:** concept → feature mapping; **phenotypes** and **value sets** (VSAC atrial-fibrillation example, Elixhauser comorbidities); **data provenance/lineage**.

**ML modeling dataset:** classical wide/long analytic tables, person-level vs person-event formats, normalization/pivoting; **feature engineering** motivations (interpretability, performance, dimensionality, NLP enablement); scaling, non-linearity, indicators, one-hot, binning, **contrast coding** (treatment, Helmert, backward difference — Pitfall 8.4.1 on destroying ordinal semantics); missing-data-aware features; supervised scores; automated FE (Deep Feature Synthesis, autoencoders, commercial tools); knowledge-based features.

**Data quality:** fitness-for-purpose; **DQD/OHDSI**-style conformance/completeness/plausibility rules; quantitative + qualitative assessment; Pitfall 8.5.1 (unexamined bias invalidates models).

**Missing data:** MCAR/MAR/MNAR taxonomy; complete-case, single/multiple imputation; informative missingness indicators.

**Data processing stacks & pipelines:** FAIR/TRUST principles; SQL, Spark, R, Python, Julia, Matlab comparison (Table 4); pipeline reproducibility (recreate modeling dataset from scratch); phenotype metadata versioning; quality gates in pipelines; documentation (Jupyter/narrative mix) — BP 8.7.1–8.7.4.

**Part B — Evaluation (book ch. 9, bundled).** Three evaluation levels: (1) **predictive performance**; (2) **generalizability** (time, geography, severity); (3) **clinical objective & cost**. Diagnostic-test vignette motivates level mismatch.

**Classification metrics:** contingency matrix; complementary pairs (sensitivity/specificity, PPV/NPV, precision/recall); Pitfall 9.1.1 (redundant related metrics); Pitfall 9.1.2 (accuracy under prevalence shift); **weighted confusion matrices**; **concordance**; **ROC/AUC**; **Lorenz curves** (clinical interpretation advantages, population comparability limits); **PR curves**; multi-class one-vs-one/one-vs-rest.

**Continuous outcomes:** residual metrics (MSE, MAE, MedAE); R² and concordance analogues; outlier/homoscedasticity pitfalls (Examples 9.1.2–3).

**Time-to-event:** censoring; Harrell's C; IBS/Brier; time-dependent ROC/iAUROC (Heagerty-Zheng strategies).

**Calibration:** weak (intercept/slope), moderate (flexible curves), strong; Hosmer-Lemeshow limits; **ICI**, E50/E90; synthetic calibration examples 9.1.4–6.

**Clinical usefulness:** weighted sensitivity/specificity; effectiveness measures (OR, RR, ARR, NNT with pitfalls); **net benefit** and **decision curves** (Vickers-Steyerberg); relationship NB↔AUC; Pitfall 9.2.3 (good AUC, bad NB vs treat-none).

**Health economic evaluation:** CEA, CUA (QALY, DALY, HYE), CBA, CMA; ICER thresholds; opportunity costs; intermediate endpoints caution.

**Error estimation & model selection (§9.4):** plug-in bias (Pitfall 9.4.1); holdout, CV, **nested CV**, bootstrap, **0.632/0.632+**; bias correction; imputation **only on training folds** (BP 9.4.7); hyperparameter tuning inside outer loops (BP 9.4.8–9); lock final model after error estimation (BP 9.4.9); pseudo-prospective validation (BP 9.4.10).
