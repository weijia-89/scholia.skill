## scope

**Black-box vs white-box monitoring**; metrics (aggregation, labels, cost); **SLIs/SLOs/error budgets**; burn-rate alerting; dashboard taxonomy; **on-call** culture and postmortems. Longest chapter in Part V slice.

---

## key_findings

### Monitoring evolution

- Black-box: up/down, synthetics for external/user view (9387–9415).
- White-box: app instrumentation (statsd/Etsy) for root cause (9391–9395).
- Synthetics catch DNS-down scenarios invisible internally (9417–9420).

### Metrics (§31.1)

- Time series + labels; label cardinality explosion risk (9423–9436).
- Emit load, internal state, dependency latency (9438–9443).
- Event-per-failure expensive at scale; **pre-aggregation** at ingest/client (9506–9550).
- Tradeoff: can't re-aggregate finer after coarse rollup (9551–9555).

### SLIs (§31.2)

- Ratio: good events / total events (0=broken, 1=perfect) (9576–9602).
- Measure closest to **user experience** (client-side preferred) (9604–9611).
- **Percentiles** over average for latency; long-tail hurts revenue (Akamai) and thread capacity (Little's Law example: 1% at 20s doubles threads) (9613–9667).
- Taming tail latency improves average and resilience (9669–9674).

### SLOs (§31.3)

- Acceptable SLI range; **error budget** = tolerated failures (9677–9692).
- Budget exhaustion → prioritize repairs over features (9694–9700).
- Multiple window sizes; start comfortable, tighten with confidence (9717–9733).
- >3 nines costly with diminishing returns; 100% impossible for user path (9731–9733).
- Stakeholder agreement; SLO must win prioritization debates (9746–9753).
- **Chaos testing** prevents dependency on undocumented behavior (9755–9762).

### Alerts (§31.4)

- Actionable alerts only; SLO/error-budget based (9766–9789).
- Precision vs recall tradeoff (9791–9799).
- Naive 1h window on 30d SLO burns only 0.14% budget — useless page (9801–9814).
- **Burn rate** alerts; multi-threshold severity (SRE workbook) (9816–9835).
- Temporary alerts for known unfixed failure modes (e.g. memory leak restart) (9837–9844).

### Dashboards (§31.5)

- Audience-first: SLO summary, public API, service-internal (9865–9918).
- Version-control dashboard definitions; top charts most important (9921–9935).
- Consistent time resolution/range per dashboard; min series per chart (9939–9961).
- Emit 0 on no-error for gap-free charts (9973–9978).

### On-call (§31.6)

- Developers operate what they build; compensate on-call (9982–9999).
- Mitigate first (rollback, scale), root-cause later; postmortem when budget burned (10010–10032).
