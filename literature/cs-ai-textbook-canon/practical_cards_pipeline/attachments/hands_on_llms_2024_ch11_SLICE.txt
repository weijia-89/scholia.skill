## scope

Chapter 11 covers **fine-tuning encoder/representation models** (not generative): full BERT classification, layer freezing ablations, SetFit few-shot, continued pretraining via MLM, and token-level NER — all with Hugging Face `Trainer`.

**Major arcs within assigned slice (lines 11981–13221):**

1. **Supervised classification** — joint fine-tune BERT + classification head vs Ch. 4 frozen models (lines 12001–12170).
2. **Freezing layers** — classifier-only F1 0.63; partial freeze (blocks 0–9) F1 0.80; full fine-tune F1 0.85; Figure 11-7 early-layer training near full performance (lines 12172–12351).
3. **SetFit few-shot** — 16 samples/class → 1,280 pairs; `all-mpnet-base-v2`; F1 0.836 on Rotten Tomatoes; zero-shot via synthetic label sentences (lines 12353–12592).
4. **Continued pretraining (MLM)** — domain-adapt `bert-base-cased` on movie reviews; fill-mask shifts from generic to movie terms; 10 epochs in `TrainingArguments` (text also mentions 20 epochs — inconsistency) (lines 12594–12806).
5. **NER** — CoNLL-2003; BIO tagging; subword label alignment (`align_labels`); `AutoModelForTokenClassification`; seqeval F1; pipeline inference (lines 12808–13171).
6. **Summary** — forward to Ch. 12 generative fine-tuning (lines 13173–13221).

**Prerequisites cited:** Ch. 2 (frozen classifier head), Ch. 4 (frozen pretrained classification), Ch. 10 (contrastive learning for SetFit).

**Manifest note:** Encoder fine-tune procedural path; generative SFT/PEFT deferred to Ch. 12 + `ai_engineering_2025` Ch. 7 framework.

## key_findings

### Supervised BERT classification

- Ch. 4 used frozen pretrained models; this chapter **updates BERT weights + classification head jointly** (lines 12017–12042).
- Dataset: Rotten Tomatoes — 5,331 positive + 5,331 negative reviews (lines 12046–12055).
- Model: `bert-base-cased`; `AutoModelForSequenceClassification`; `DataCollatorWithPadding`; F1 via `load_metric` (lines 12066–12114).
- Training: lr 2e-5, batch 16, 1 epoch, weight_decay 0.01 → **eval F1 0.849** vs Ch. 4 task-specific **0.80** (lines 12127–12169).
- Fine-tuning "only costs a couple of minutes" on example hardware (lines 12169–12170).

### Layer freezing ablations

| Configuration | eval F1 | Notes |
|---------------|---------|-------|
| Full fine-tune | 0.849 | Baseline |
| Classifier head only | 0.633 | Faster training |

| Figure 11-7 | ~full with 5 blocks | Diminishing returns |

(lines 12172–12344)

- Frozen layers should be followed by trainable layers (lines 12225–12227).
- Multi-epoch training amplifies freeze vs full gap (lines 12346–12351).

### SetFit few-shot

- SetFit (Tunstall et al. 2022): 3 steps — pair sampling, contrastive embedding fine-tune, classifier on embeddings (lines 12367–12465).
- In-class pairs: n·(n−1)/2 positives; cross-class negatives (lines 12408–12417).
- 16 samples × 2 classes = 32 docs → **1,280 unique pairs** (20 iterations × 2 polarities) (lines 12477–12541).
- `all-mpnet-base-v2` base; 3 contrastive epochs → **F1 0.836** on full test set (lines 12488–12575).
- Zero-shot SetFit: synthetic sentences from label names (e.g., "The example is happy") (lines 12585–12592).

### Continued pretraining (MLM)

- Three-step genealogy: pretrain → **continue MLM on domain** → task fine-tune (BioBERT pattern) (lines 12606–12639).
- `AutoModelForMaskedLM`; `DataCollatorForLanguageModeling` mlm_probability=0.15; token vs whole-word masking trade-off (lines 12649–12700).
- Fill-mask before domain adapt: "horrible idea/dream/day"; after: "horrible movie/film/comedy" (lines 12759–12791).
- Load continued model: `AutoModelForSequenceClassification.from_pretrained("mlm")` (lines 12797–12806).
- Ch. 10 cross-ref: MLM as domain adaptation before embedding training (lines 11437–11443).

### Named-entity recognition

- Token-level classification; CoNLL-2003 ~14k training samples; BIO labels (B-PER, I-PER, B-ORG, etc.) (lines 12810–12914).
- Subword alignment: first subtoken gets B-, continuations I-; special tokens labeled -100 (lines 12966–13031).
- `AutoModelForTokenClassification`; `DataCollatorForTokenClassification`; seqeval overall F1 (lines 13031–13084).
- Pipeline: `"My name is Maarten."` → B-PER/I-PER on subtokens (lines 13137–13171).
- NER useful for **de-identification and anonymization** of sensitive data (lines 12814–12815).
