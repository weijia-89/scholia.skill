## scope

Chapter 9 (text lines **40902–41848**) surveys **deep learning computer vision in dermatology and dermatopathology** as of ~2022. Covers CNN/DNN fundamentals, transfer learning, input modalities (clinical photos, dermoscopy, whole-slide images), problem types (classification, segmentation, weakly supervised pathology), landmark human-vs-model comparison studies, U-Net segmentation pipelines, WSI patch→tissue→slide hierarchical classifiers, scanner/staining bias pitfalls, ISIC datasets/challenges, augmented intelligence framing, and gaps (few prospective trials, limited prognosis/therapy prediction from morphology).

Out of scope: exhaustive tutorial on CNN theory (points to Murphree et al. JAMA Dermatology reviews and Goodfellow/LeCun texts).

## key_findings

### Foundations

1. **Augmented intelligence** — Authors favor human–computer synergy over autonomous replacement; cross-ref "Human with Machine" chapter. [verified, 40958–40964]

2. **CNNs dominate dermatology AI** — Convolution + pooling for translation-invariant hierarchical features; common architectures: ResNet, DenseNet121, EfficientNet, GoogleNet. [verified, 40970–41003]

3. **BP 15.1** — Start from known SOTA architecture (EfficientNet as fast signal probe); do not design custom nets first. [verified, 41005–41009]

4. **Supervised learning prevalence** — Cancer diagnosis, risk stratification, lesion ID; large data requirements. [verified, 41011–41017]

5. **Transfer learning** — Pretrained networks reduce data needs; standard in published dermatology DL. [verified, 41018–41029]

6. **BP 15.2 / Pitfall 15.1** — Use transfer learning when similar pretraining exists; if only ImageNet-like weights, also try training from scratch (especially pathology). [verified, 41031–41041]

### Data types and problem framing

7. **Input modalities** — Clinical vs dermoscopic vs WSI; patient-captured vs clinic photos are distinct distributions. [verified, 41052–41062]

8. **BP 15.3** — Check outcome balance by image acquisition source when mixing sources. [verified, 41064–41070]

9. **Classification vs segmentation** — Image-level labels vs pixel labels; combined pipelines (segment then classify). [verified, 41072–41082]

10. **Weak supervision in pathology** — Slide-level labels from EHR reduce pixel-annotation burden (BP 15.4). [verified, 41084–41095]

### Classification literature

11. **Melanoma/cancer focus** — Majority of AI work on pigmented lesions; inflammatory disease underrepresented. [verified, 41108–41113]

12. **Landmark comparisons** — Esteva et al. (Nature 2017) CNN vs 21 dermatologists on biopsy-proven sets; Han, Codella, Brinker, Haenssle et al. man-vs-machine designs with varying dermatologist experience and clinical context integration. [verified, 41115–41166]

13. **Mobile triage** — Soenksen et al. (2021) wide-field phone photos, >90% SPL sensitivity. [verified, 41168–41175]

14. **Robustness gaps** — Ethnicity/domain shift (Han 2018); ensemble and OOD corruption benchmarks (Gessert, Maron); class imbalance handling (Sayed et al.). [verified, 41177–41197]

15. **Prospective evidence scarce** — As of 2022, only one noted prospective clinical trial (Han et al. 2022 RCT). [verified, 41199–41204]

### Segmentation

16. **Pixel-level difficulty** — Hair, fuzzy borders, color variation favor CNN over handcrafted methods. [verified, 41206–41222]

17. **Encoder–decoder and U-Net** — Dominant architecture; skip connections preserve detail; Jaccard >80% cited as reliability threshold (Ashraf et al. 2022). [verified, 41223–41266]

### Dermatopathology / WSI

18. **BP 15.5 patch paradigm** — Patch tissue classifier (DNN) → slide-level aggregator (often logistic regression to limit overfitting). [verified, 41276–41290]

19. **Pitfall 15.2 scanner effects** — Models learn scanner/stain/protocol artifacts; multicenter rare-disease concentration is red flag. [verified, 41292–41303]

20. **WSI study patterns** — Mitosis detection (Moreno-Andrés), BCC/nevus/SK classification (Olsen), Spitz vs conventional melanocytic (Hart: 99% curated vs 52% whole-slide patches), Hekler melanoma vs pathologists on crops, weakly supervised MPATH triage (Sankarapandian), interpretable multi-class WSI segmentation (Thomas). [verified, 41311–41367]

21. **Prognosis gap** — Limited work linking WSI morphology + genetics to therapy response/survival. [verified, 41370–41377]

### Datasets and outlook

22. **ISIC Archive and challenges** — Central public resource with known limitations; annual challenges at vision conferences (BP 15.6). [verified, 41379–41397]

23. **Conclusion** — Dermatology image-centric; ~3000 skin diseases, large global burden; opportunity for remote specialty access. [verified, 41399–41409]
