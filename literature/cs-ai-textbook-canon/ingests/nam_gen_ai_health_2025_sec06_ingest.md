## Scope

The appendix summarizes rapidly evolving GenAI governance landscapes (WHO, EU AI Act, U.S. federal policy, municipal guidelines, industry partnerships) and provides an **implementation guide** with workshop background, publication takeaways, and stakeholder-specific application/strategy matrices for providers, payers, regulators, public health, tech industry, patients/advocates, and medical education.

## Key findings

All claims **[verified from text]** from lines 2362–2581.

### Rapid field development (2363–2413)

- Parallel acceleration of GenAI and governance complexity affects agencies, societies, and data subjects (2365–2369).
- **Global:** WHO six principles for AI governance in health; EU AI Act comprehensive framework (European Union, 2023; WHO, 2021) (2371–2374).
- **U.S. federal:** AI Bill of Rights five principles (White House, 2022); Biden EO on safe/trustworthy AI (White House, 2023) (2375–2382).
- **Local/municipal:** e.g., City of San Jose GenAI guidelines balancing opportunity with bias/privacy/cyber risks (2383–2384).
- **Industry:** partnerships and direct investment for technology access (2385–2387).
- Expect increasingly detailed GenAI-specific governance as health applications expand (2388–2390).
- Stakeholder alignment (developers, health systems, government, advocacy, patients) essential for ethical/safe regulation (2391–2408).
- Reiterates transformative applications: CDS, admin burden reduction, drug discovery, patient education (2409–2413).

### Implementation guide — project background (2436–2446)

### Paper overview takeaways (2447–2470)
