## 1) TL;DR

* Sentence form must perform its meaning — length, rhythm, punctuation, and diction should enact the content, not merely describe it. Corroborated by Tufte's Artful Sentences and independently described as "iconic signification" in craft-theory literature. \[verified — Tufte 2006 via archive.org, Poets and Writers summary\]
* Fragments are emphatic compression devices for sensory detail, pivotal moments, and memory. LLMs avoid fragments and irregular syntax \[verified — Vollmer field guide; Reinhart et al. 2025 PNAS shows reduced syntactic diversity in instruction-tuned models\]. Fragment insertion is a deai differentiator, but the original claim "RLHF text never uses fragments" is overstated — retagged \[OVERSTATED\] and scoped to "RLHF-default output produces deliberate rhetorical fragments at rates far below literary prose."
* The compound sentence produces a steady, authoritative rhythm. GPT-4o specifically avoids clausal coordination \[verified — Reinhart et al. 2025 PNAS\]. Llama 3 variants use it more than humans \[verified — same source\]. The original claim "RLHF defaults to complex sentences; compound sentences are underrepresented" is model-specific, not a universal RLHF property — retagged \[OVERSTATED\].
* List sentences are a compression technique. This is Long's pedagogical heuristic with no external empirical test found. \[speculative — no falsifying or confirming evidence located\]
* Paragraph-type variation counters RLHF direct-paragraph defaults. LLM paragraph structure defaults to topic-sentence-first, 4-beat arc, and uniform paragraph length \[verified — Bloomberry AI Sentence DNA analysis; Vollmer field guide; Pangram Labs detection guide\]. Long's four-type taxonomy is heuristic, not exhaustive, but the underlying claim that LLMs default to direct paragraphs is externally corroborated.

### Three strongest hostile-expert objections

1. The ingest attributes RLHF-specific behavioral signatures (fragment avoidance, compound-sentence underrepresentation, passive overuse, direct-paragraph defaulting) to a monolithic "RLHF" without acknowledging that these behaviors are model-family-specific and generation-dynamics-dependent. Reinhart et al. 2025 shows Llama base models closely resemble human text, while instruction-tuned models diverge — and even among instruction-tuned models, GPT-4o and Llama Instruct differ on clausal coordination, passive voice, and downtoner use. The ingest's RLHF claims are not claims about RLHF per se but about GPT-style instruction tuning specifically.

Resolution: C-002, C-004, C-010 scope-narrowed. All RLHF references in craft moves now qualified as "instruction-tuned LLM defaults" with model-family specificity noted.

2. Long is a Tier-3 source (practitioner) whose claims are pedagogical heuristics, not empirical findings. The ingest originally marked claims as "STRONG" without distinguishing between source-text faithfulness (which is high) and external evidential support (which is weak-to-moderate). A corpus-linguistics hostile expert would note that Long cites zero empirical studies and her examples are all survivorship-biased toward canonical published writers.

Resolution: All craft moves now carry explicit confidence scores. No claim exceeds 60. Source tier is acknowledged throughout. "STRONG" ranking retained only for craft moves with external corroboration; others downgraded to "MODERATE" or "MODERATE-LOW."

3. The phonesthesia claim (CM-07/C-007) is presented as if Long's specific diction-matching advice (sharp words for tense content, mellifluous for soft) is empirically grounded. Cross-linguistic phonesthesia research supports sound-symbolism for narrow semantic domains (size, shape, force) but not for the broad aesthetic correspondence Long describes. The ESL scope limit is correctly flagged but underweighted.

Resolution: C-007 confidence capped at 50. Mechanism relabeled from "phonesthesia" to "phonesthesia-adjacent craft intuition." ESL scope limit elevated to primary constraint.

### What could not be verified

* Fragment frequency in LLM output: No study specifically measures deliberate rhetorical fragment rates in instruction-tuned model output vs. literary prose baselines. A corpus analysis comparing fragment frequency in GPT-4o/Claude output vs. a matched literary corpus would resolve C-002 decisively.
* Compound-sentence frequency in literary prose vs. LLM output: Reinhart et al. measures "clausal coordination" (a Biber feature) but this is not identical to Long's definition of the compound sentence. A study using Long's grammatical definition would be more precise.
* Paragraph-type distribution in LLM output: No study classifies LLM paragraphs using Long's four-type taxonomy. The Bloomberry 4-beat arc maps approximately but not exactly to Long's "direct" type.
* Imitation efficacy (C-011) and single-clause-type training efficacy (C-013): No controlled studies found. These are testable with pre/post writing samples from student populations.
* Lanham and Williams convergence with Long: Not checked this review. Would strengthen or weaken C-014.

## 7) Epistemic notes (revised)

* Source tier: Tier 3 (practitioner knowledge). Long is an experienced writing teacher. Claims are pedagogical heuristics supported by literary examples, not controlled studies. Confidence ceiling for practitioner-only claims: 45.
* Survivorship bias: All examples are from published, canonized writers. The failure distribution is invisible. Long partially discloses this: "first attempts invariably give you cliches."
* External verification conducted: Reinhart et al. 2025 (PNAS, PMC11874169) — peer-reviewed, open-access, Biber-feature analysis of 6 LLMs vs. human text, N=66,320+ text chunks. Primary external source for all RLHF-behavioral claims. Tufte 2006 Artful Sentences — confirmed via archive.org and doi.org/10.1353/sty.2018.0042. Cross-linguistic phonesthesia — confirmed via 4+ studies (Blasi et al., De Gruyter 2018, PMC 6433836, ACL 2025). End-weight principle — confirmed via 3 independent sources (doi.org/10.1017/s1360674316000356, ACL 2023, journals.sagepub.com). AI paragraph defaults — confirmed via Bloomberry, Vollmer, Pangram Labs (Tier 3 sources, converging).
* Key correction: The original ingest's RLHF-behavioral claims treated "RLHF" as a monolithic behavioral signature. External evidence shows LLM stylistic behavior is model-family-specific and generation-dynamics-dependent. GPT-family and Llama-family models diverge on clausal coordination, passive voice, and downtoner use. All deai claims now carry model-family qualifiers.
* Gaps remaining: Fragment frequency quantification, Lanham/Williams convergence check, paragraph-type taxonomy mapping to AI defaults, imitation efficacy, single-clause-type training efficacy.
