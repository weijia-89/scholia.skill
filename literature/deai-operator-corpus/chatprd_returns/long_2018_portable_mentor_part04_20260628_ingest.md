## 1) TL;DR (≤5 bullets — strongest claims first)

* Clichés are dead metaphors that populate first drafts; the craft move is recognition-and-removal in revision, not avoidance during generation. Application to LLM output (where no human revision pass occurs) is \[inferred\], not stated by Long.
* Revision sequence matters: "deepening, extending, elaborating, and only then burnishing and honing" — polishing thin work is premature optimization. \[verified against source\]
* Mixed metaphors "usually" (Long's hedge) signal the writer is unaware of being figurative; A→B comparison discipline violated. \[verified against source\]
* Some subjects defy comparison entirely; "the practice of making metaphor ought to include the practice of eschewing metaphor." Hardest principle to operationalize; Long provides only one example (Neruda). \[verified against source\]
