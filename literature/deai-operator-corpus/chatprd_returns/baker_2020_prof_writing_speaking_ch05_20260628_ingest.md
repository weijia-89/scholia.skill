## Chapter Summary (analytical, not paraphrase)

Ch05 presents a four-pass document review framework (DOCS) and embeds two sub-frameworks for content review (Five C's, CLOUD) and one for sentence-level style review (SPELL). The chapter also covers three professional editing phases and practical revision tactics. All frameworks are presented as pedagogical scaffolding (mnemonic acronyms for introductory students), not as empirically validated methods.

### Core Frameworks

DOCS — four sequential review passes:

1. Design — visual appeal, formatting compliance, HATS elements (Headings, Art/graphics, Typography, Spacing)
2. Organization — approach (direct vs. indirect), information structure (hierarchy + sequence), writing pattern (OABC — referenced, not defined in ch05)
3. Content — Five C's + CLOUD paragraph audit
4. Style — formality, tone, social sensitivity, SPELL sentence-level check

Five C's of content quality:

* Clear — familiar words, logical flow, appropriate explanations
* Complete — answers all relevant 5W2H questions; no missing info, no irrelevant excess
* Correct — truthful, provable, accurate; free of bias, exaggeration, defamation; citations verified
* Considerate — appropriate formality, positive/neutral tone, social sensitivity, "you" attitude
* Convincing — the "ultimate test" per Baker; achieves intended purpose from audience perspective

CLOUD paragraph standards:

* Cohesion — linking words, backward/forward/relationship cohesion statements
* Length — appropriate; long paragraphs flagged for splitting
* Organization — topic sentences present; subsequent sentences appropriately sequenced
* Unity — all sentences relate to the paragraph topic
* Development — adequate statistics, examples, explanations for audience comprehension

SPELL sentence-level audit:

* Structure — subject, verb, complement
* Punctuation — comma usage identified as most frequent error area
* Errors — grammar and usage (case, agreement, tense, mood, voice, numbers, capitalization)
* Language — spelling, word choice
* Length — conciseness; excessive sentence length flagged

### Three Professional Editing Phases

1. Substantive editing — overall message content, strategy, structure; making the document functional for its audience
2. Copyediting — writing style fixes; grammar, punctuation, spelling errors
3. Proofreading — typographical and mechanical problems; formatting errors

### Revision Effectiveness Tactics

* Remember purpose, audience, context; read through audience's eyes
* Wait a day after drafting before reviewing
* Read text aloud (Baker's rationale: silent reading clusters words, misses individual-word problems)
* Use software grammar/style checkers (Grammarly cited by name)
* Use proofreaders' marks for paper review; track changes for electronic review
* For critical documents, get feedback from knowledgeable, honest reviewers
* Give reviewers specific instructions; vague feedback ("looks pretty good") is not useful
* Usability testing for instructional documents
* Use the D/O/C/S margin-note method to organize review comments

### Pedagogical Devices

* Running example: SPM property management audit memo reviewed across all four DOCS passes (Figures 5.4–5.8)
* Figure 5.2: standard proofreaders' marks
* Figure 5.3: D/O/C/S feedback organization format
* Figure 5.8: fully revised memo showing before/after transformation
* End-of-chapter rubric (Design, Organization, Content, Style — 10-point scale each)

## ESL Preservation Notes (post-adversarial, precision-revised)

\[esl_preserve\] Baker's "considerate" criterion assumes US business-English warmth norms. However, Baker's framework also independently values "clear" and "correct" communication. L1-Chinese directness — shorter sentences, less hedging, fewer performative politeness markers — may score high on "clear" and "correct" while registering differently on "considerate." The pathologizing risk is real but specific: it arises only when a reviewer equates directness with inconsiderateness, conflating two of Baker's own distinct criteria. Defense: point to Baker's own Five C's as separable dimensions.

\[esl_preserve\] Baker's "read aloud" tactic may surface L1-influenced syntax (e.g., topic-comment structure, article omission). These are not revision targets unless they independently correlate with RLHF residue. Do not conflate ESL voice features with writing errors or with AI-generated patterns.
